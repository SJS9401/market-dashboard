SUMMARY

Seagate Technology Holdings (STX 6.87%) directly raised its annual revenue growth target to at least 20% due to accelerating demand and visibility on nearline contracts through 2027. Executives emphasized the rapid adoption and ramp of Mozaic 4 drives and momentum in qualifying Mozaic platforms with major cloud customers, resulting in HAMR exabyte output expected to become dominant by late 2026. Debt reduction was prioritized, with substantial early retirement of convertible notes, and strong free cash flow generation allowed a concurrent focus on shareholder returns. Product mix shifts toward higher capacity drives and operational cost control materially improved profitability, with non-GAAP gross margin and operating margin both reaching all-time highs.

  • Seagate cited finalized build-to-order contracts and value-based pricing agreements as supporting sequential profit and revenue gains through fiscal 2027.
  • Management sees sustained pricing power as “there are no changes to our pricing strategy” and forecast year-over-year data center revenue per terabyte to continue rising as “this trend to continue, supported by a strong demand environment.”
  • Mozaic 5's development remains “well to plan,” targeting 50-terabyte drives for late 2027, leveraging in-house photonics to extend aerial density.
  • Executives confirmed unit shipments are not growing materially; instead, increasing exabyte output per unit is prioritized by maximizing aerial density and component efficiency.
  • Gianluca Romano said, “the vast majority of our nearline capacity is allocated during the next 4 quarters” [fiscal 2027], providing high revenue predictability.
  • Fitch’s upgrade to investment grade reflects financial discipline and margin expansion achieved ahead of management’s prior targets.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • Exabyte: A unit of digital storage equivalent to one billion gigabytes, used to quantify very large-scale data shipments or installations.
  • HAMR (Heat-Assisted Magnetic Recording): Advanced hard drive writing technology that enables higher data density per disk, core to Seagate's latest product generation.
  • Mozaic Platform: Seagate’s proprietary HAMR-based mass-capacity hard drive architecture representing their latest high-density product family.
  • Nearline: A storage category optimized for mass capacity and frequent access—primarily supplied to data centers and large cloud service providers.
  • Build-to-Order Contracts: Customer agreements specifying future product configurations, supply allocation, and pricing for enhanced predictability and planning.
  • Areal Density: The amount of data stored per unit area on a hard disk platter, a key metric for increasing storage efficiency and value.
  • Agentic AI: Artificial intelligence workflows in which autonomous agents continuously generate and store large, unstructured, and structured data streams, heightening storage demand.
  • RPO (Remaining Performance Obligations): A forward-looking metric indicating the total value of contracted revenue yet to be recognized, cited by management as a proxy for future demand from cloud customers.

And so therefore, it really needs to be well orchestrated in our supply chain. It's not like just plugging in a few more machines to get more capacity out.

Our people are very much focused on increasing the aerial density, the amount that comes out of the entire fleet, that's the way we believe gets the most exabytes into the world. This is the way we're driving and trying to be as aggressive as we can.

Gianluca Romano: I'll say the move to the second generation HAMR now is giving us the opportunity to continue to grow and to achieve a target CAGR that we discussed about a year ago.
